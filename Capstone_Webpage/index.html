What are Sharps?

“Sharps is a medical term for devices with sharp points or edges that can puncture or cut skin. They may be used at home, at work, and while traveling to manage the medical conditions of people or their pets, including allergies, arthritis, cancer, diabetes, hepatitis, HIV/AIDS, infertility, migraines, multiple sclerosis, osteoporosis, blood clotting disorders, and psoriasis.” – Food and Drug Administration (FDA)
In addition, sharps are also used for recreation use of drugs, homeopathic medicinal treatments such as acupuncture, and even hobbyist who sew in their home, or place of work.

What are Sharp Containers (Drop Box)?

A sharps container, or sharps drop box is a device used for safe disposal of sharps including needles, syringes and lancets. Although sharps containers are available through clinics and pharmacy’s, this method is inconvenient for the daily sharp user. Placing drop boxes in neighborhoods and around the city in public places allow better access to dispose of sharps safely rather than disposing of them in the trash where they can be a hazard for sanitation engineers, and other types of employees that handle garbage. Safe methods of disposal in sharps containers can reduce the amount of needle stick injuries (NSI) in the workplace.

History of Sharps in Seattle, WA

  • August 2016

    The Sharps Pilot Program was esablished in Seattle, WA. This program started to respond to citizen complaints about sharps discards in the public right of way. Several reports claimed discards were found in parks, including dog parks, buisness owners found needles near doorways where they were forced to change the light near the entrance to a blue or red light to prevent needle disposal. As there was a rise in needle disposal in the public right of ways, there was also a rise in needle stick injuring among sanitation engineers, and employees who handle garbage.
