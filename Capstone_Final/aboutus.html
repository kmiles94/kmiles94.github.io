SPU Sharps Program

Background:
Seattle Public Utilities (SPU) department of Illegal Dumping saw the effects of injection drug usage through discarded sharps (needles, syringes, and lancets) in the public right of way. Complaints of discarded needles in public spaces continued to rise within the city. While many of these complaints are thought to be from illicit drug usage, some are likely from individuals who use needles for medical reasons. Feedback from Seattle residents via phone calls, e-mail, and the city’s mobile reporting app led to the start of the Sharps Collection Pilot Program.

The Pilot Program:
The Sharps Collection Pilot program, founded in 2016, has the goal of reducing the number of sharps (syringes, needles, lancets) in public spaces while increasing safety measures surrounding needle disposal. The pilot provides services in the form of six large outdoor sharps disposal boxes throughout the city, along with smaller disposal boxes located in comfort stations in three city parks. In addition, the pilot provides removal services for sharps found on public property within 24 hours. The sharps collection staff work comes from complaints from our website, phone calls, and the city’s mobile reporting app called the Find It, Fix It app. While these services are incredibly helpful for public property, the Sharps Collection staff does not pick up needles on private property. To help mitigate issues of improper needle disposal on private property, the pilot also provides baseline resources, education, and community trainings for customers on sharps cleanup. As of January 2, 2019, SPU has collected 17,940 sharps from complaints. A total of 137,449 sharps have been collected from the drop box locations since February of 2017. This amounts to a total of 155,389 sharps collected since the program’s inception.

Program Goals:
The goals of the program in 2018 were to continue to monitor the nine disposal box locations, respond to complaints within 24 hours, expand education and outreach efforts, and survey the community to identify public opinion. The team collected and organized data from complaints and from the drop boxes, monitored feedback from customers, organized education events, and conducted outreach in many Seattle neighborhoods. As the year went on, the education and outreach efforts expanded within SPU, as well as out in the community. A 2018 highlight for the Sharps program was the completion of a series of educational videos on the program.
